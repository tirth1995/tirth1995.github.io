CURRENT COURSES

  • TIS 3630: Business Info Systems: This course explores the role of information systems in modern business environments. It covers database management, enterprise resource planning (ERP), and decision support systems. Students learn how businesses use technology to manage operations, analyze data, and improve efficiency. Key topics include database design, SQL, cloud computing, and business analytics. The course also highlights how organizations leverage data-driven insights to make strategic decisions, enhance customer relationships, and optimize supply chain management.

  • CSC 5750: Principles of Web Technology: This course introduces students to web development tools, frameworks, and best practices for building modern websites and web applications. Topics include HTML, CSS, JavaScript, responsive design, and front-end frameworks like React or Angular. The course also covers back-end technologies, including server-side scripting, APIs, and databases. Students gain hands-on experience by developing dynamic, interactive websites while learning about web security, accessibility, and performance optimization. The goal is to equip students with the skills needed to create functional and user-friendly web applications.
  • CSC 4320: Systems Administration: This course teaches students how to configure, manage, and maintain server systems in various IT environments. It covers operating systems (Windows, Linux), user account management, networking, virtualization, and system security. Students learn to set up and troubleshoot servers, implement access controls, and automate administrative tasks using scripting. The course also introduces cloud-based solutions and best practices for maintaining system reliability and performance. By the end of the course, students will be prepared to manage IT infrastructures in business and enterprise settings.
  • CSC 5272: Principles: Cyber Security: This course provides an in-depth understanding of cybersecurity principles, focusing on encryption, network security, and threat prevention. Topics include cryptographic techniques, firewall configurations, intrusion detection systems, and ethical hacking concepts. Students explore how cyber threats evolve and how security professionals defend against attacks such as ransomware, denial-of-service (DoS) attacks, and data breaches. The course also examines security policies, risk management, and compliance with industry standards. By the end, students will have the foundational knowledge needed for careers in cybersecurity and ethical hacking.
